# Product Concept

Capsule Wishes is a reflective iOS app for people who want to reconnect with their wishes, notice meaningful coincidences, and cultivate small daily joy without turning personal growth into pressure.

## Product Positioning

Capsule Wishes sits between:

- a private journal;
- a gentle goal companion;
- a ritual builder;
- an emotional self-reflection tool.

It should not feel like a task manager, a horoscope app, or a therapy replacement. The desired feeling is closer to a private night-sky room where the user can place something important and return to it over time.

## Emotional Promise

The app helps the user:

- formulate wishes when they do not fully know what they want;
- give a wish emotional form;
- notice small signs, coincidences, and joys;
- take tiny actions without pressure;
- understand whether a wish was fulfilled, transformed, or released.

## Target Users

Primary users:

- people who enjoy journaling, reflection, rituals, symbolism, and personal meaning;
- people who want to work with goals in a softer, more emotional way;
- people who feel anxious, tired, or disconnected from their desires and need a low-pressure tool for noticing life.

Secondary users:

- creators and writers who want a personal intention archive;
- people working with long-term dreams;
- people who like atmospheric, private, emotionally intelligent apps.

## User Journey

### 1. First Launch

The user enters a short story-like onboarding:

- childhood wishes;
- the idea that every person has a personal ritual;
- the distinction between passive wishing and attentive movement;
- the invitation to create a first capsule.

### 2. Wish Creation

The app helps the user express the wish through prompts:

- What do you want?
- How will you know it happened?
- What feeling are you looking for?
- What are you afraid to admit you want?
- When should this capsule open?

Inputs may include:

- text;
- voice note;
- image;
- color;
- symbol;
- opening date.

### 3. Sealing

The sealing flow is a short ceremony. The wish enters a capsule with visual feedback: light, particles, gentle movement, and a sense of preservation.

### 4. Active Capsule

The capsule becomes a living object. The user can:

- add a sign;
- add a small joy;
- add a thought;
- complete a ritual;
- record a small action;
- reflect on how the wish feels now.

### 5. Opening

When the opening date arrives, the user sees:

- the original wish;
- journal entries linked to it;
- rituals completed;
- small actions taken;
- reflection prompts.

The result is not binary. Possible outcomes:

- fulfilled;
- in progress;
- changed;
- released;
- needs another cycle.

## Key Screens

### Onboarding

Purpose: create emotional context and trust.

Elements:

- atmospheric full-screen visuals;
- short narrative copy;
- first capsule invitation;
- no heavy feature explanation.

### Capsule Constellation

Purpose: show all wishes as a living personal sky.

Elements:

- floating capsule objects;
- opening countdowns;
- subtle status glow;
- quick create button;
- daily reflection entry point.

### Capsule Detail

Purpose: show one wish as a living intention.

Elements:

- capsule visualization;
- opening date;
- current ritual;
- recent signs, thoughts, dreams, and steps;
- small actions;
- reflection button;
- opening state when ready.

### Journal

Purpose: train attention.

Fast entry types:

- Strange thing;
- Thought;
- Dream;
- Tiny step.

Example prompts:

- What felt a little strange today?
- What made today one percent lighter?
- What did you notice that seemed connected?
- What did you survive or complete today?
- What did your body seem to know before your mind did?

### Ritual Library

Purpose: help users discover their personal way of relating to wishes.

Rituals should be short, gentle, and optional. They should support attention and action, not superstition or dependency.

### Archive

Purpose: preserve the user's path.

Elements:

- opened capsules;
- fulfilled wishes;
- changed wishes;
- released wishes;
- recurring themes;
- personal ritual patterns.

## Voice and Tone

The app should sound:

- warm;
- poetic;
- calm;
- honest;
- non-clinical;
- non-manipulative.

Avoid:

- guaranteed outcome language;
- guilt;
- fake urgency;
- overly mystical claims;
- motivational aggression.

Examples:

- "A wish can change shape when you finally listen to it."
- "Today does not need to be bright to contain one small light."
- "You do not have to force the sign. Just notice what stayed with you."
- "A small step still counts when it was taken gently."

## Mental Well-Being Boundaries

Capsule Wishes may support reflective habits, gratitude, and emotional awareness. It must not position itself as a treatment for anxiety, depression, trauma, or any mental health condition.

Important product rules:

- no crisis handling unless implemented with proper safety flows;
- no diagnosis;
- no claims of clinical effectiveness;
- no shame-based retention;
- no pressure to write every day;
- clear encouragement to seek professional support when needed.
